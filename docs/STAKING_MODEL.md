# Canton Staking Protocol: Economic Model

This document outlines the core economic mechanics of the Canton Staking Protocol, including reward calculation, the unbonding process, and slashing conditions. The protocol is designed to secure the network by incentivizing token holders to stake their assets with reliable validators.

## 1. Overview

The protocol involves two primary participants:

*   **Validators:** Entities that run Canton nodes, participate in network consensus, and create transaction blocks. They lock up a self-bond and accept delegations from token holders.
*   **Delegators:** Token holders who delegate their stake to a validator to participate in the network's security and earn rewards, without needing to run their own node.

Delegators choose a validator and stake tokens to their pool. The collective stake of a validator (self-bond + delegations) contributes to their weight in the network. Rewards and penalties are applied to the entire pool and distributed proportionally.

## 2. Staking and Reward Calculation

Rewards are distributed on a per-epoch basis. An epoch is a fixed time interval, for example, 24 hours.

### Key Terms

*   **`TotalNetworkStake`**: The sum of all tokens staked across all validator pools in the network.
*   **`PoolStake`**: The total amount of tokens staked with a single validator, including the validator's self-bond and all delegations.
*   **`DelegatorStake`**: The amount of tokens an individual delegator has staked with a validator.
*   **`EpochReward`**: A predetermined amount of tokens minted or allocated from a treasury to be distributed as rewards for a given epoch.
*   **`ValidatorCommission`**: A percentage fee that the validator charges on all rewards generated by their pool. This fee is set by the validator.

### Reward Formula

The reward calculation for a single delegator within a single epoch follows these steps:

1.  **Calculate the Pool's Share of Rewards:** A validator's pool earns a share of the `EpochReward` proportional to its stake relative to the `TotalNetworkStake`.

    `PoolReward = EpochReward * (PoolStake / TotalNetworkStake)`

2.  **Calculate Validator Commission:** The validator takes their commission from the `PoolReward`.

    `ValidatorCommissionAmount = PoolReward * ValidatorCommission`

3.  **Calculate Distributable Rewards:** The remaining rewards are distributed to all participants in the pool (the validator and all delegators).

    `DistributablePoolReward = PoolReward - ValidatorCommissionAmount`
    or
    `DistributablePoolReward = PoolReward * (1 - ValidatorCommission)`

4.  **Calculate Individual Delegator's Reward:** Each delegator receives a share of the `DistributablePoolReward` proportional to their stake in the pool.

    `DelegatorReward = DistributablePoolReward * (DelegatorStake / PoolStake)`

### Reward Distribution

Rewards are automatically compounded. At the end of each epoch, earned rewards are calculated and added directly to the delegator's existing stake in the pool, increasing their `DelegatorStake` for subsequent epochs.

## 3. Unbonding Process

To withdraw staked tokens, a delegator must initiate an unbonding process. This is a multi-step procedure designed to prevent abrupt changes in the total network stake and enhance stability.

1.  **Unbond Request:** The delegator submits a request to unbond a specific amount of their staked tokens.
2.  **Unbonding Period:** Once the request is made, the tokens enter a **21-day unbonding period**.
3.  **Withdrawal:** After the 21-day period has passed, the delegator can execute a final `Withdraw` transaction to transfer the unbonded tokens back to their wallet.

### State During Unbonding

During the 21-day unbonding period, the tokens have the following properties:
*   They **do not** earn rewards.
*   They **are still** subject to slashing if the validator they were delegated to commits a slashable offense during this period.

## 4. Slashing Conditions

Slashing is a penalty mechanism that removes a portion of a validator's pool stake in response to misbehavior. This penalty affects the validator's self-bond and all of its delegators' stakes proportionally. The goal is to create a strong economic disincentive against actions that harm the network's integrity, liveness, or security.

Slashed funds are transferred to a protocol-controlled treasury.

### Liveness Faults (Downtime)

*   **Condition:** A validator fails to participate in consensus for an extended period. This is typically measured by missing a high percentage of required signatures within a defined window (e.g., more than 95% of signatures missed over a 10,000-block window).
*   **Penalty:** **0.5%** of the total `PoolStake` is slashed. This is a relatively minor penalty to punish poor performance without being overly harsh for temporary, unintentional outages.

### Equivocation (Double-Signing)

*   **Condition:** A validator signs two different, conflicting blocks at the same block height. This is a severe security infraction as it can be used to attempt to fork the chain or double-spend assets.
*   **Penalty:** **5.0%** of the total `PoolStake` is slashed. This severe penalty reflects the critical nature of the offense and is intended to be a powerful deterrent.
